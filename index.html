Collatz and birthday paradox calculator

The Collatz conjecture, named after Lother Collatz, holds that when applied to any positive value of n, it will always reach 1: If n is even, it is divided by two. If n is odd, it is multiplied by three and then added one.

The birthday paradox, also known as the birthday problem, states that in a random group of 23 people, there is about a 50 percent chance that two people have the same birthday.
